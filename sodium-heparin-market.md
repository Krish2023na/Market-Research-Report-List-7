<p><p>Sodium Heparin is a widely used anticoagulant derived from animal tissues, primarily porcine intestinal mucosa. It is commonly employed in medical settings to prevent and treat blood clots, including deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, and during surgeries like dialysis and cardiac procedures. Its mechanism of action involves enhancing the activity of antithrombin III, which inhibits clotting factors.</p><p>The Sodium Heparin Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for anticoagulant therapies, rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, and advancements in healthcare infrastructure.
