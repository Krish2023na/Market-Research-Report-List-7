<p><p>Fabry Disease therapeutic market comprises two primary types: Enzyme Replacement Therapy (ERT) and alternative therapies. ERT involves administering synthetic forms of the deficient enzyme alpha-galactosidase A, targeting the root cause of the disease and alleviating symptoms. Alternative therapies may include substrate reduction therapies and chaperone therapies, aiming to lessen substrate accumulation or stabilize the malfunctioning enzyme, respectively. Both approaches seek to improve patient outcomes, enhance quality of life, and address the unique challenges posed by Fabry Disease management.</p></p>
