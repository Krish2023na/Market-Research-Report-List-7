<p><p>Hyperspectral remote sensing encompasses various spectral bands, primarily categorized into three types: VNIR (Visible Near Infrared), SWIR (Shortwave Infrared), and LWIR (Longwave Infrared). VNIR captures wavelengths ranging from 400 to 1000 nm, suitable for vegetation and mineral analysis. SWIR, covering 1000 to 2500 nm, is effective for detecting water content and soil types. LWIR, extending beyond 8000 nm, is used for thermal mapping, identifying surfacesâ€™ heat emissions, and monitoring environmental changes. These technologies cater to diverse applications in agriculture, environmental monitoring, and resource management.</p></p>
