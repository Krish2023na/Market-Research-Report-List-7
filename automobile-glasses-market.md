<p><p>The automobile glass market includes **tempered glass**, **laminated glass**, and **others**. Tempered glass is heat-treated for strength, shattering into small, blunt pieces for safety. It’s commonly used in side and rear windows. Laminated glass consists of two glass layers bonded with a plastic interlayer, offering durability and shatter resistance, ideal for windshields. Other types include specialized glasses like acoustic, solar control, or smart glass, catering to advanced features like noise reduction, UV protection, or adaptive tinting, enhancing vehicle safety and comfort.</p></p>
