<p><p>The gas barbecue grill market is primarily divided into two types: Liquid Propane (LP) and Natural Gas (NG) barbecues. LP grills use portable propane tanks, offering versatility and convenience for outdoor cooking in various locations. They are popular among campers and tailgaters. In contrast, NG grills are connected directly to a natural gas line, providing a continuous fuel source and eliminating the need for tank replacements. They are ideal for home use, especially where permanent setups are preferred.</p></p>
