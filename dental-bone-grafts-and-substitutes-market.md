<p><p>The dental bone grafts and substitutes market includes several key types: Demineralized Bone Matrix (DBM), which is derived from human bone and retains essential proteins for healing; Xenograft, sourced from animal bones, typically bovine, providing a scaffold for new bone formation; and Synthetic grafts, made from biocompatible materials that mimic natural bone properties. Each type serves to enhance bone regeneration in dental procedures, offering various biocompatibility and resorption rates to meet specific clinical needs.</p></p>
