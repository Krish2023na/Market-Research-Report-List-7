<p><p>The floor sanding tool market encompasses two primary types: dry polishing and wet polishing. Dry polishing uses abrasive pads to smooth surfaces without additional liquids, making it ideal for quick jobs and minimizing cleanup. In contrast, wet polishing incorporates water or other fluids, reducing dust and providing a cooling effect, which enhances the polish's finish and prolongs tool lifespan. Both methods cater to different preferences and flooring materials, influencing their adoption in residential and commercial settings.</p></p>
