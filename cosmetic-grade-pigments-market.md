<p><p>The cosmetic grade pigments market comprises two primary types: water-soluble pigments and oil-soluble pigments. Water-soluble pigments are typically used in formulations like liquid foundations, lip glosses, and blushes, as they disperse easily in water-based products. In contrast, oil-soluble pigments are utilized in formulations such as lipsticks, creams, and powder products, where they blend seamlessly with oil-based components. Each type offers distinct color properties and application methods, catering to varied cosmetic product requirements and consumer preferences.</p></p>
