<p><p>The photochromic dyes market includes various types such as azobenzenes, spiropyrans, diarylethenes, fulgides, hexaarylbiimidazole, and spiroperimidines. Azobenzenes exhibit reversible light-induced isomerization, making them useful in optical switches. Spiropyrans change color under UV light, ideal for sensors and smart materials. Diarylethenes offer high thermal stability and fatigue resistance, suitable for data storage. Fulgides are known for their photochromic reversibility and durability. Hexaarylbiimidazole is used in imaging and photoresists. Spiroperimidines provide unique color changes, often applied in coatings and textiles. Each type serves specific industrial applications based on its photoresponsive properties.</p></p>
